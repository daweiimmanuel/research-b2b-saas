We're running closed loops on paid creative at Single Grain and it's borderline unfair.

I need to explain what I mean because this is the thing I'm most fired up about right now.

Most companies are using AI like a search bar. Ask Claude a question, get an answer, paste it somewhere, close the tab. Tomorrow you open a fresh chat and start over. Nothing carries forward.

That's an open loop. You're the glue holding it together, and the second you step away, it stops.

A closed loop is different. The output of one cycle becomes the input for the next. The system teaches itself.

Here's how it works for us on paid creative.

We feed in everything we know about the customer and the offer alongside the channel data. Agents spin up hundreds of ad variants. We launch. Performance data comes back. And instead of me reading a report and writing a new brief, the loop reads the data itself, scales winners, kills losers, saves what worked, and runs again. Every cycle starts smarter than the last.

Nobody resets to zero. Nobody briefs from scratch.

We have agents named Oracle and Picasso. Oracle pulls performance data. Picasso builds the creative. I'm sitting in a Slack thread calling shots while these things run laps around what a traditional team could do in a week.

And we're still early. New skills, new agents going in every week. The gap between companies building loops and companies still prompting ChatGPT one question at a time is going to be massive by the end of this year.

If you're running a business and you haven't thought about closed loops yet, start now. This is where the leverage lives.
