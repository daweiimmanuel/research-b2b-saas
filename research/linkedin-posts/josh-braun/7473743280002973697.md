Most cold email subject lines sound like marketing.

"Increase Revenue"
"Transform Your Sales Process"
"Unlock Growth"

The problem?
There is no problem. 

Brains are hard wired to pay attention the problems. It's why we avoid stepping in dog poop. 

Or risks.

Or things someone wants to avoid.

Examples:

Nonprofit:
Low annual donors

Controller:
Missing receipts

VP Sales:
Stalled deals

RevOps:
CRM adoption

Customer Success:
ALEX renewal risk

Manufacturing:
FL Production delays 

HR:
High AE turnover

IT:
Security gaps

Dealership:
Slow lead response

CFO:
Budget overruns

Why do these work?

Because they don't sound like they're trying to get your attention.

They sound like something already on your attention.

The best subject lines don't promise a better future.

They hint at a problem someone is already worried about.

Think less like a marketer.

Think more like a coworker sending an internal email.
