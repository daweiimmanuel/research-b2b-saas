I was sitting in the sauna.

Outside, it's hot.
Inside it's cold.

There was Ruby.
Lying by the doorway.

Not in the heat.
Not in the room with me.
Just close enough.

Dogs are funny that way.

They don't seem to care much about what you're doing.

Reading a book.
Working at your desk.
Walking to get the mail.
Sitting in a wooden box sweating for reasons that make absolutely no sense.

What they care about is where you are.

I looked through the glass and there she was.

Curled up on the stone.
Watching the garden.
Occasionally glancing toward the door.
Waiting.

Not because she wanted anything.

Not because she expected anything.

Just because being near me was enough.

And if you've ever had a dog, you know exactly what I'm talking about.

The sound of paws following you from room to room.

The head resting on your foot while you work.

The way they wait by the window when you're gone.

The way they greet you like you've returned from war after a trip to the grocery store.

Dogs have a way of making you feel important without ever saying a word.

As I watched Ruby lying there, I realized something.

For most of our lives, we're chasing attention.

Dogs give us something much rarer.

Their presence.
Undivided.
Uncomplicated.
Unconditional.

No agenda.
No scorekeeping.
No "what have you done for me lately?"

Just:

"You're my person. I'll be right here."

And one day, when she's gone, what i'll miss most isn't the barking or the walks or the routines.

It'll be those quiet moments.
Looking through a door.

And seeing someone who simply wanted to be close to you. 🐾❤️
