One of the most useful things I've learned:

Never take things personally.

A few years ago, my wife and I were on a bike tour in Napa Valley.

Our guide seemed irritated from the moment we arrived.

Short answers.
Little patience.
The kind of energy that makes you wonder if you did something wrong.

I spent the first part of the tour trying to figure it out.

Does he not like us?
Did I say something?
Why is this guy so annoyed?

Then he told us his son had died of cancer at 28.

The loss was still fresh.

Nothing about his mood had anything to do with me.

I had walked into the middle of a story I knew nothing about.

The rude customer.
The prospect who sounds annoyed.
The person who cuts you off in traffic.
The stranger who seems angry at the world.

We assume we're the cause.

Usually we're just seeing the surface of a struggle we can't see.

Everyone is carrying something.
Some burdens are visible.
Most aren't.

Remember that, and it's easier to respond with compassion instead of judgment.

Most things aren't personal.
They're pain with nowhere else to go.
