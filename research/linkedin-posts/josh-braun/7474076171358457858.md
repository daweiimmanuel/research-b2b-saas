Prospects don't want to feel judged.

Judgment sounds like this:
"I noticed your garage window is cracked. Why haven't you fixed it?"

"Why" puts people on the witness stand.
It asks them to justify themselves.
To explain.
To defend.

So they do.

They give surface answers.
They tighten up.
They protect their story.

Compare that to this:

"Saw your garage window is cracked. Not sure if it's on the to-do list or if it's currently competing with the other 47 things homeowners are usually dealing with." 

Same observation.
No interrogation.

It replaces why with understanding.
Assumption with empathy. 

When people don't feel cross-examined, they open up.
When they don't have to defend, they tell the truth.

If you want to unlock more truth, 
drop "why."
